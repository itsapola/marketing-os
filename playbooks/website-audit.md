# Website Audit Playbook

> A structured framework for evaluating a website's ability to communicate value, support business objectives, and drive measurable outcomes.

---

# Purpose

A website is more than a collection of pages.

It is often the first experience a customer has with a company, product, or brand.

This playbook provides a repeatable framework for auditing websites using a combination of strategic evaluation, human judgment, and AI-assisted analysis.

The objective is not to identify every possible issue.

The objective is to identify the improvements most likely to increase clarity, trust, usability, differentiation, and business impact.

---

# When to Use

Use this playbook when:

* Launching a new website
* Redesigning an existing site
* Preparing for a product launch
* Evaluating competitors
* Supporting due diligence
* Improving conversion
* Refreshing messaging
* Improving SEO
* Reviewing a portfolio site
* Performing quarterly marketing reviews

---

# Desired Outcomes

A successful audit should answer five questions:

1. Does the website clearly explain what the company does?
2. Why should someone choose this company instead of competitors?
3. Is the user guided toward the correct next action?
4. Where does friction occur?
5. What should be improved first?

---

# Step 1 — Business Context

Before evaluating the website, document:

* Company
* Industry
* Primary audience
* Business model
* Core product
* Primary conversion goal
* Main competitors
* Success metrics

Without business context, website recommendations become generic.

---

# Step 2 — Homepage Clarity

Questions:

* Can a first-time visitor explain what the company does within ten seconds?
* Is the value proposition immediately visible?
* Is the audience obvious?
* Is the primary problem identified?
* Is the solution clear?
* Is there unnecessary jargon?
* Does the page answer "Why should I care?"

Deliverable:

Homepage clarity score (1–10)

---

# Step 3 — Messaging

Evaluate:

* Positioning
* Differentiation
* Value proposition
* Tone
* Brand voice
* Emotional appeal
* Credibility
* Simplicity
* Consistency

Questions:

* Is the message memorable?
* Could this copy belong to a competitor?
* Does every section reinforce the same narrative?
* Are claims supported?

Deliverable:

Messaging strengths and weaknesses.

---

# Step 4 — Audience Fit

Evaluate whether messaging aligns with the intended audience.

Questions:

* Does the language reflect customer priorities?
* Are customer pains clearly understood?
* Does the site match the sophistication level of the audience?
* Is technical depth appropriate?
* Does the content answer likely objections?

Deliverable:

Audience alignment assessment.

---

# Step 5 — Navigation

Review:

* Menu structure
* Information architecture
* Page hierarchy
* Search
* Internal linking
* User pathways

Questions:

* Can users find key information quickly?
* Are labels intuitive?
* Are important pages buried?
* Is navigation predictable?

Deliverable:

Navigation recommendations.

---

# Step 6 — Content Quality

Review:

* Headlines
* Body copy
* Visual hierarchy
* Readability
* Formatting
* Scannability
* Redundancy
* Missing content

Questions:

* Does every section have a purpose?
* Can unnecessary copy be removed?
* Are ideas presented logically?
* Is information easy to scan?

Deliverable:

Content improvement opportunities.

---

# Step 7 — Trust Signals

Review:

* Testimonials
* Customer logos
* Case studies
* Certifications
* Security
* Team
* Contact information
* Social proof
* Awards
* Media mentions

Questions:

* Why should a visitor trust this company?
* Is credibility established early?
* Are important proof points easy to find?

Deliverable:

Trust assessment.

---

# Step 8 — Conversion

Review:

* Calls to action
* Forms
* Buttons
* Page flow
* Friction
* Microcopy
* Pricing visibility
* Lead capture
* Demo requests

Questions:

* Is the primary action obvious?
* Is unnecessary friction introduced?
* Does every page support a conversion goal?

Deliverable:

Conversion optimization recommendations.

---

# Step 9 — SEO

Review:

* Title tags
* Meta descriptions
* Heading structure
* Internal linking
* Content depth
* Schema opportunities
* Page speed
* Search intent alignment
* Keyword targeting

Deliverable:

SEO opportunities prioritized by impact.

---

# Step 10 — Accessibility

Review:

* Contrast
* Keyboard navigation
* Alt text
* Heading hierarchy
* Form labels
* Link clarity
* Readability

Deliverable:

Accessibility findings.

---

# Step 11 — Competitive Comparison

Compare against three to five competitors.

Evaluate:

* Positioning
* Messaging
* Product explanation
* Calls to action
* Trust
* Visual hierarchy
* Brand personality
* Differentiation

Questions:

* Where is this website stronger?
* Where is it weaker?
* What opportunities are being missed?

Deliverable:

Competitive scorecard.

---

# Step 12 — Prioritized Recommendations

Recommendations should be grouped by effort and impact.

## High Impact / Low Effort

Quick wins that should be implemented immediately.

## High Impact / High Effort

Strategic investments.

## Low Impact / Low Effort

Nice improvements.

## Low Impact / High Effort

Usually defer.

---

# AI Responsibilities

AI is well suited for:

* Summarizing copy
* Identifying repeated themes
* Finding inconsistencies
* Comparing competitors
* Drafting recommendations
* Scoring readability
* Suggesting alternative headlines
* Detecting missing information
* Generating executive summaries

---

# Human Responsibilities

Humans remain responsible for:

* Business context
* Strategic interpretation
* Brand judgment
* Creative direction
* Prioritization
* Stakeholder alignment
* Final recommendations

---

# Deliverables

A completed website audit should include:

* Executive summary
* Homepage assessment
* Messaging evaluation
* Audience analysis
* Navigation findings
* Content review
* Trust assessment
* Conversion review
* SEO review
* Accessibility review
* Competitive comparison
* Prioritized action plan

---

# Success Criteria

The audit is successful when another marketer can:

1. Understand the business.
2. Identify the biggest opportunities.
3. Prioritize improvements.
4. Explain why those improvements matter.
5. Execute the recommendations with confidence.
